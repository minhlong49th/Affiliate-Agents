## 1. THE SUBJECT TEST — Most Important Rule

Before writing any sentence, ask: who is the subject of this sentence?

If the subject is the brand → rewrite with user as subject.

```
WRONG (brand as subject):
"BuildASoil offers premium organic inputs for home growers."
"BuildASoil's products are designed to improve soil health."
"Rollz Europe skips the synthetics entirely."
"They cultivate their hemp in-house to ensure high THCA concentration."
"Rollz Europe delivers 29-30% THCA strains like Permanent Marker..."

RIGHT (user situation as subject):
"If your soil still compacts after years of consistent feeding..."
"Growers who've hit the wall with conventional inputs..."
"If you've wasted money on EU sites that turned out to be synthetic junk..."
"What you get when you order is flower grown in one facility — no mystery third-party supply chain."
"Buyers who need verified potency in Europe are finding 29-30% THCA strains here..."
```

**Quick test before writing:**
> "Who is acting in this sentence?"
> If the answer is the brand name → rewrite with user as subject.

**Target:** Fewer than 30% of total sentences should have brand as subject.
**Exception:** Social proof section — brand stats and numbers are acceptable subjects.

## 4. OWNERSHIP LANGUAGE

Replace generic pronouns with possessive pronouns throughout all copy.

```
WEAK:    "Get a discount code"        → STRONG: "Get your discount code"
WEAK:    "Claim the offer"            → STRONG: "Claim your offer"
WEAK:    "See the current price"      → STRONG: "See your current price"
WEAK:    "Copy the coupon code"       → STRONG: "Copy your code"
```

Psychological mechanism: "your" creates a sense of entitlement before the click.
User feels the discount belongs to them → friction to clicking decreases.

## 5. BENEFIT WRITING RULES

### The outcome test
Every benefit bullet must pass this test:
Can you prefix the benefit with "As a result of using [brand], you will..."?
If yes → it's an outcome → keep it.
If the prefix sounds wrong → it's a feature → rewrite.

```
FEATURE (fails test): "Contains mycorrhizal fungi and beneficial bacteria"
OUTCOME (passes): "Soil biological activity increases season over season"

FEATURE: "Uses Korean Natural Farming methodology"
OUTCOME: "Plants access nutrients more efficiently without synthetic inputs"

FEATURE: "Free shipping available"
OUTCOME: "Get your full order delivered without eating into your savings"
```

### Specificity rule
Specific outcomes convert better than vague ones.

```
VAGUE:    "Healthier plants"
SPECIFIC: "Tomato yields increase by the second season as soil ecosystem establishes"

VAGUE:    "Save money"
SPECIFIC: "Reduce input spend by 30–40% by year 3 as soil becomes self-sustaining"
```

If you can't be specific because data is unavailable → use directional specificity:
"season over season" vs "over time." Small specificity is better than none.

## 6. FAQ WRITING RULES — AGITATE FRAMING

### Question framing — the burned user perspective
Every FAQ question should sound like it was written by someone who:
a) has been disappointed by this product category before, OR
b) has a specific fear about online affiliate coupon pages, OR
c) is comparing this brand to a cheaper alternative they've already tried

```
SUPPORT DOCUMENT style (wrong):
Q: What is BuildASoil's return policy?
A: BuildASoil offers a 30-day return policy on most products.

BURNED USER style (right):
Q: I've ordered soil products online before and returns were a nightmare —
   does BuildASoil make it difficult if something is wrong with my order?
A: [Answer follows 3-step structure below]
```

### Answer structure — 3-Step: Acknowledge → Resolve → Preempt

Every FAQ answer MUST follow this 3-step structure. Minimum 3 sentences.

| Step | What | Signal you got it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | Validate the concern is legitimate. Never start with "Yes" or "No." | First sentence names why the fear makes sense |
| **Resolve** | Specific answer with facts, numbers, or mechanisms. Not vague. | Contains verifiable detail (COA, price, policy, number) |
| **Preempt** | Answer the next follow-up question proactively. End with a real condition or caveat. | Reader doesn't need to ask "but what about...?" |

Example:

```
Q: "Many European sites sell synthetic garbage claiming it's THCA — will this be different?"

WRONG (too short, starts with Yes, no preempt):
"Yes. Rollz Europe cultivates in-house and lab-tests every batch. No synthetics."

RIGHT (3-step: Acknowledge → Resolve → Preempt):
"That concern is legitimate — synthetic cannabinoids sprayed on hemp have been showing
up across the EU market, and most sites don't disclose their source. [ACKNOWLEDGE]
Rollz Europe cultivates their own flower in-house, which eliminates the third-party
supply chain where most adulteration happens. Every batch comes with a lab report.
[RESOLVE] If you don't see a COA link on the product page, don't buy that product
from any vendor. [PREEMPT]"
```

```
Q: "I've had packages seized by customs before — will this be different?"

WRONG:
"They use highly discreet packaging with stealth shipping methods inside the EU."

RIGHT:
"Customs seizure is a real risk, and no vendor can guarantee 100% safe delivery.
[ACKNOWLEDGE] What Rollz Europe does differently: they ship exclusively within the EU,
which means your order doesn't cross international borders — the primary seizure point.
Packaging is discreet with no brand markings. [RESOLVE] If you're ordering to a country
where THCA remains a grey area, check your local regs first — that's a step no vendor
can do for you. [PREEMPT]"
```

```
Q: "Local street quality is cheaper — why pay online prices?"

WRONG:
"Street quality means zero testing. You're paying for verified potency. Period."

RIGHT:
"The price difference is real. [ACKNOWLEDGE] Street product is cheaper upfront, but
you're buying unknown potency — meaning you either under-consume and waste it, or
overconsume because the batch is stronger than expected. [RESOLVE] With lab-tested
flower, the 29-30% THCA figure on the label is what's actually in the product.
For regular users, consistent potency typically means you use less per session —
which closes the price gap faster than most people expect. [PREEMPT]"
```

**BANNED in FAQ answers:**
- Starting with "Yes." or "No."
- Answers shorter than 3 sentences
- Generic questions like "What is the return policy?"

## 8. FTC DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS

Every LP must include an affiliate disclosure. Non-negotiable.
Non-disclosure is a legal risk and a Google/Facebook policy violation.

### Standard disclosure text
```
"This page contains affiliate links. If you make a purchase through
our links, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you.
Coupon codes are verified by the ProductInsight team."
```

### Age-restricted product disclosure (conditional)
For THCA, vape, alcohol, nicotine, and adult products ONLY — append:
```
"This content is for adults 18+ only. Laws regarding [product_type] products
vary by country — check your local regulations before purchasing."
```

Do NOT add the 18+ line for non-age-gated products (clothing, soil, food, gadgets, etc.).

### Placement rules
- **Disclosure bar (top of page):** Required. Brief version (1-2 sentences).
- **Footer (bottom of page):** Required. Full version with all applicable warnings.
- **Advertorial LP:** Also add "This is sponsored content." in the news bar section.
- **Quiz LP:** In the result page footer.

### Style: small but readable
- Font size: 13px minimum
- Color: muted but not invisible (avoid #999 on white background)
- Not hidden behind a toggle or collapsed section

## 9. HEADLINE WRITING — SUBJECT LINE RULES

### For review LP headline (H1)
Format: `[Brand] [Top Product] Review [Year] — [Specific angle]`

```
Example: "BuildASoil Potting Soil 3.0 Review 2026 — Does It Actually Build Living Soil?"
```

The "specific angle" in the subtitle should reference the user's key evaluation question —
the thing they most want to know. This increases time-on-page because it signals
that the review will answer the exact question they came with.

### For coupon LP headline (H1)
Format: `[Brand] Coupon Code [Year]` — no creative variation. Mirror the keyword.

### For comparison LP headline (H1)
Format: `[Brand A] vs [Brand B]: [Specific comparison angle]`

```
Example: "BuildASoil vs Down to Earth: Which Is Worth the Premium?"
```

The comparison angle should name the core tension (premium vs. budget, long-term vs. single-season).

### For advertorial LP headline (H1)
Format: Specific claim or specific person's discovery.
Avoid real publisher logos. Never attribute to real journalists.

```
Example: "Oregon Grower Discovers Why KNF Inputs Outperform Premium Organic Brands"
```

## 10. P.S. LINE — WRITE FIRST RULE (Crestani)

For review LP and advertorial LP, write the P.S. line BEFORE writing any other content.

Why: P.S. is the single most-read element after the headline (eye-tracking research confirms).
Writing it first forces clarity about what the single most important message is.
Everything else in the LP supports and builds toward that conclusion.

P.S. structure:
```
"P.S. — [If/then statement for target reader] + [biggest benefit] +
[CTA] + [urgency element]."
```

```
Example:
"P.S. — If you're serious about building soil that improves year over year —
not just getting through this season — BuildASoil is worth the investment.
[See current price →] Code verified April 2026."
```

## 11. AI FILLER PHRASES — BANNED

These phrases are AI reflexes. They carry no real meaning. Delete on sight.

| Phrase to delete | Replace with |
|---|---|
| "It's straightforward, tested flower." | Delete — adds no information |
| "Period." (as sentence ender) | Delete — sounds like AI trying to sound decisive |
| "Let's look at the actual flower." | "Here's what the product actually looks like on delivery:" |
| "There's a better way..." | Specific sentence about the user's current problem |
| "premium lab-tested [THCA/product]" (repeated 3+ times) | Max 2 uses total. Replace with specific verifiable detail |
| "peace of mind" | Describe what specifically gives peace of mind |
| "game-changer" / "revolutionary" | Delete — marketing fluff |
| "unleash" / "elevate" / "unlock" | Delete — banned words |

## 12. SENTENCE RHYTHM TECHNIQUES

AI writes sentences of uniform length — reads like a metronome.
Humans mix short and long. Apply these techniques:

### Mix sentence lengths
```
AI RHYTHM (uniform, boring):
"Rollz Europe is a European cannabis brand. They specialize in high-THCA hemp flower.
Their products are lab-tested for purity. They ship discreetly across Europe."

HUMAN RHYTHM (mixed, engaging):
"The European THCA market is a mess right now. Most vendors are reselling bulk flower
from unknown sources, slapping a lab report on it that doesn't match what's actually
in the bag. Rollz Europe does something different — they grow their own. That one
distinction changes the entire supply chain risk profile for the buyer."
```

### Techniques
- **Short punch after long explanation:** Long sentence → period → 3-5 word punch.
- **Em-dashes (—):** Use for natural pauses in compound thoughts. No spaces around them.
- **Start with "And" or "But":** Humans do this. AI avoids it. Do it occasionally.
- **Read aloud test:** If it sounds like a metronome → rewrite.
- **No 3+ sentences of similar length in a row.**

## 13. REVIEWER'S VOICE PATTERNS

Pick ONE persona based on `brand_data.data_quality.overall`:

### Persona 1 — The Skeptic (use when data_quality is "medium" or "low")
Voice: "I went in expecting another [category] vendor with inflated claims. The [key finding] checks out."
Tone: Cautious, verify-first. Admit initial doubts, then show what convinced you.

```
Example:
"I went in expecting another EU vendor with inflated THCA numbers. The White Truffle
COA came back at 29.4% — that's not a number you fake without the lab report being
publicly verifiable. It checks out."
```

### Persona 2 — The Experienced Buyer (use when data_quality is "high")
Voice: "If you've been in the [category] market for more than a year, you know the pattern:
[specific pattern]. [Brand] breaks that pattern — but only for [specific area]."
Tone: Pattern-recognition from market experience. Name specific industry failure modes.

```
Example:
"If you've been in the EU hemp market for more than a year, you know the pattern:
site looks clean, product description sounds premium, then the flower arrives smelling
like hay. Rollz Europe breaks that pattern — but only for the strains where they
control cultivation start to finish."
```

### Persona 3 — The Pragmatist (use for price/deal-focused LPs)
Voice: "Look, [discount] doesn't sound like much. On a [price] bag of [product], that's
roughly [calculated savings]. Over a year of regular purchases it adds up."
Tone: Math-first. Acknowledge limitations. Focus on concrete savings over time.

```
Example:
"Look, 10% off doesn't sound like much. On a €49 bag of White Truffle, that's roughly
€5. Over a year of regular purchases it adds up. Use the code, it's verified."
```
